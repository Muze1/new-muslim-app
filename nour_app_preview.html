FAQ & quick tips
Common questions for new Muslims
💡 Tip of the day
Start with what you can manage. Even saying "Bismillah" before meals is a beautiful act of worship. Every small step counts in the sight of Allah.
Do I need to know Arabic to pray? ▼
You should learn the Arabic words of prayer, but it takes time. Many scholars allow new Muslims to pray in their own language at first while learning the Arabic. The most important thing is to begin praying and learn gradually.
What breaks my wudu? ▼
Wudu is broken by: using the bathroom, passing wind, deep sleep, loss of consciousness, and (according to most scholars) touching private parts directly. If any of these occur, you must repeat wudu before praying.
Can I pray if I miss the prayer time? ▼
Yes — if you miss a prayer due to forgetfulness or sleep, you should pray it as soon as you remember. There is no sin for an honest missed prayer, but you should make it up promptly and try to establish a routine going forward.
Do I need to tell my family I converted? ▼
This is a personal decision. Islam does not require you to announce your conversion publicly. Many new Muslims find it helpful to connect with a local Muslim community first for support before telling family.
What is halal food and do I have to eat it? ▼
Halal refers to food that is permissible in Islam. The main restrictions are pork and alcohol. Meat should ideally be slaughtered in the Islamic way. Many scholars make allowances for new Muslims adjusting to these changes gradually.
🤲 Remember
Islam is a journey, not a destination. Allah knows your heart and your effort. Be patient with yourself and trust that with sincerity, He will guide your steps.
